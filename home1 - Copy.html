Cyber Security

What is Cyber Security?

Cybersecurity is the protection of computer systems and networks from information disclosure, theft of or damage to their hardware, software, or electronic data, as well as from the disruption or misdirection of the services they provide.

Cybersecurity Domains

A strong cybersecurity strategy has layers of protection to defend against cyber crime, including cyber attacks that attempt to access, change, or destroy data. Extort money from users or the organization, or aim to disrupt normal business operations.

Commom Cyber Threats

Ransomware- A type of malware that locks down files, data or systems, and threatens to erase or destroy the data.

Phishing- A form of social engineering that tricks users into providing their own PII or sensitive information.

Malware- Provide unauthorized access or cause damage to a computer. Malware attacks are designed to get around familiar detection methods, such as antivirus tools.

Insider Threats- Invisible to traditional security solutions like firewalls and intrusion detection systems, which focus on external threats.

DDoS attack- Attempts to crash a server, website or network by overloading it with traffic, usually from multiple coordinated systems.

APT- An intruder or group of intruders infiltrate a system and remain undetected for an extended period.

Man-in-the-middle- An eavesdropping attack, where a cybercriminal intercepts and relays messages between two parties in order to steal data.
